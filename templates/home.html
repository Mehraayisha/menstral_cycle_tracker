Sometimes it can feel embarrassing talking about periods – but remember, periods are normal. You are entitled to ask for the help that you need to experience good menstrual health and wellbeing. A period is the part of the menstrual cycle when women, girls and people who menstruate bleed from their vagina. For most people this happens every 28 days or so, but it’s common for periods to start sooner or later than this. It can range from day 24 to day 38 of your cycle. Periods last from around 4 to 8 days. If you’re aged 26 or under or are looking for information to help a young person, you can find out more about periods on the Young Scot website. You can get period products for free in Scotland in a range of places. You can contact your local council to find out where you can access free products in your area or you can use the PickupMyPeriod app. By law, councils and education providers now have to make these products available for free for anyone who needs them.
